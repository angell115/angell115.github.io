Katana are typically made from a special type of steel called tamahagane, which is produced from iron sand. The steel is heated and repeatedly folded to create a blade that is both strong and flexible. The final product is a work of art, with a distinctive hamon (temper line) that showcases the blade's unique characteristics.

These swords were historically used by the samurai, the warrior class of feudal Japan, and are often associated with the bushido code of honor and discipline. The process of making a katana is highly specialized, involving multiple stages of forging, folding, and tempering the steel to create a blade that is both resilient and sharp.
